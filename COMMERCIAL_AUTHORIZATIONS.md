### Legal Framework

The entries in this document are specific waivers or supplementary licenses appended to the primary CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 license. Each authorization is legally binding and satisfies the written permission requirement of the NonCommercial clause.

**General Conditions Applying to All Authorized Entities:**
1.  **Attribution:** Mandatory credit must be given to **renarchi** as the original author in all distributions, documentation, and user interfaces where applicable.
2.  **Attribution Chain:** Where sublicensing rights are explicitly granted, the authorized entity must ensure that all sublicensees are equally bound by the attribution requirement. The obligation to credit **renarchi** passes through every sublicense link.
3.  **No Transfer of Ownership:** These permissions grant the right to *use* and *monetize* the work; they do not transfer intellectual property ownership. **renarchi** remains the sole author and intellectual property owner of the original model weights at all times, regardless of the scope of any authorization granted. No authorized entity may claim authorship, ownership, or original creator status over the model or its weights.
4.  **Scope:** Each authorization applies only to the models explicitly named in the relevant entry. Unlisted models remain under CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 without commercial exception.
5.  **Conditional Validity:** Each authorization is valid only for as long as its stated conditions — including attribution — are continuously met. Persistent, uncorrected breach of the attribution requirement renders the authorization void for the period of non-compliance.

### Requesting Commercial Authorization

If you wish to be added to this document or need a commercial license for a specific project, contact **renarchi** via:
*   **GitHub Issues/Discussions**

When submitting a request, please describe:
- The model(s) you wish to use commercially.
- Your product or service name, what it does, and a link if available.
- How the model will be integrated (bundled in software, hosted inference, API, etc.).
- How users access the product (subscription, one-time purchase, per-use billing, etc.).
- Whether end users will have direct access to the model weights.
- Whether you require sublicensing or redistribution rights.
